University of Pennsylvania Tutor

During the school year, I work one-on-one with Penn students, helping them understand concepts and problem-solving strategies for calculus and computer science programming classes. I usually work with about four students per semester, and I meet with each student once a week.

  

Penn Emerging Scholars Program Teaching Assistant

The Penn Emerging Scholars Program works to increase the presence of women and minorities in math and computer science fields at the University of Pennsylvania. Once a week, I teach an introductory lesson to topics in math and computer science, like cryptography, graph theory, the Pigeon Hole Principle, and more. Because the group is primarily aimed at those who fall within minority groups that have been historically discouraged from STEM, PESP creates an environment where these students may explore STEM where they might not have been able to previously.

Art

Write On! Coordinator

Write On! is an organization I hold very near to my heart. I joined Write On! in the first weeks of my Freshman year at Penn, and have been involved ever since. Write On! consists of a group of Penn students who work with students of an under-resourced, low-income middle school in West Philadelphia. Each Friday for about three hours, we write creatively with middle school students. We work with them to write poems, tell stories, build a character, choreograph a dance, write a rap, and express themselves through a creative medium. In my third year, I began coordinating the program, which means I write the weekly syllabi that the coaches (what we call the Penn students) use to guide their work with the kids. I also created and maintain the Write On! website and social media so that the kids can view and share their work at any time.
