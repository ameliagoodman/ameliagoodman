Feminist Paraphenalia

A multi-media series: a canvas tote bag, a t-shirt, and a poster. The canvas tote references Judith Butler's conception of gender as performance, as well as Ru Paul's Drag Race a favorite show of mine. In the queen's waves reads "gender is performance." The t-shirt juxtaposes stereotypical girlish cartoons and realistic images from a young woman's life: high heels, long lashes, a bunny contrasted with bloody tampons, a joint, an electric guitar. The answer to Beyonc√©'s question: "Who run the world?" is of course "GIRLS." I drew and then silk screened both the tote and t-shirt. Finally, the poster addresses a gendered accusation of being too loud, having too many feelings: "Uppity." I used various fonts and framing to surround the word to print through the letterpress. I intended for the romanticism of the letterpress to juxtapose the insulting nature of the word.
