Diversity in STEM

I founded Tech It Out Philly in the Spring of 2016 as a semester-long course for Philadelphia public high school students. We work with 15 students to create websites that address some social issue of their choosing. At the end of the semester, we hold a “launch party” for students to bring family and friends to show off all they have done.

"Addressing Social Issues through Web Development: Getting Marginalized Youth Excited about Computer Science and Social Change"

I also conducted a study of marginalized youth and computer science education in Philadelphia, using Tech It Out Philly as a model. I examined an attempt to address the lack of educational CS opportunities for Philly public schoolers--particularly in groups of students that have been historically marginalized--and how to help foster social activism in young minds.

FemmeHacks is a women’s collegiate hackathon that aims to create a welcoming environment for new and experienced hackers. FemmeHacks allows over 200 women to explore their passion for tech by hacking on web, mobile, and hardware to create projects from scratch over the weekend. I organized FemmeHacks in 2016 and 2017, specializing in the hacker experience and logistical organization.
