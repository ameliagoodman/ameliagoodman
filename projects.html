Some projects that I’ve been working on include:

• Tech it Out Philly: Tech it Out Philly is a semester-long course for Philadelphia publich high school stduents. 15 students learn how to create websites that address some social issue of their choosing. At the end of the semester, we hold a “launch party” for students to bring family and friends to show off all they have done.

• FemmeHacks: FemmeHacks is a women’s collegiate hackathon that aims to create a welcoming environment. FemmeHacks is available to all women, beginner to experienced, and allow women to explore their passion for tech by hacking on web, mobile and hardware to create projects from scratch over the weekend. This is FemmeHacks’ third year, and I along with two other women organize and host this event at Penn.

• Mentorship for Women in CIS: In the fall of 2015, I began a mentorship program for women majoring in CIS at Penn. This program matches every freshman and sophomore woman who intends to major in CS with an older woman also majoring in CS. This allows underclass women to feel more comfortable in the male-dominated major, and creates a closer bond among women in CS at Penn. This model is expandable to other universities and high schools, as mentorship has been a proven to be an effective part of retention of women in tech and contribution to women’s happiness in the field.

• Write On!: Write On! is a children’s literacy program that I coordinate with a few other Penn students. Write On! is funded by the Kelly Writers House at the University of Pennsylvania. We work with the Lea Elementary school to bring Penn students and West Philadelphia youth together in a meaningful way by writing creatively together.
